Drishyam 3 is a 2026 Indian Malayalam-language crime drama film written and directed by Jeethu Joseph. Produced by Antony Perumbavoor for Aashirvad Cinemas, it is a sequel to Drishyam 2 (2021) and the third installment in the Drishyam film series. The film stars Mohanlal, alongside Meena, Ansiba Hassan, Esther Anil,  Kalabhavan Shajon, Siddique, Murali Gopy, and Asha Sarath.
Principal photography began on 22 September 2025 and concluded on 2 December 2025. Drishyam 3 was released worldwide on 21 May 2026. Unlike its predecessors, the film received mixed reviews from critics.


== Plot ==

The plot of the film connects with its predecessors. For more information, see Drishyam and Drishyam 2.
Georgekutty adapts the novel Drishyam into a highly successful film. As he is enjoying the success of the film, he is approached by journalists, Yami and Roni, regarding links between the film's plot and his own life. Georgekutty denies any such connection though the journalists continue to investiage Varun's death, intending to make a documentary on it. Later, Georgekutty is approached by Jose and Mary, seeking forgiveness for Jose's actions. Georgekutty tells that what Jose did was not wrong and provides them with the finance to start a new life.
At home, Georgekutty and Rani are trying to find a suitable partner for Anju through their trusted marriage broker Mathayi. However, as soon as a good proposal arrives, the party is persuaded against it by someone anonymous. Mathayi gives the number of the caller to Georgekutty, who with the help of retired police officer Suresh Babu, identifies the callers to be Rajan and his wife Santha. Rajan was suspended for aiding Georgekutty and the financial problems that followed led to the stop of his daughter's education and her attempting suicide, leading to her losing her voice. Rajan patiently waited for Georgekutty to help him but in the midst of his battle to save his family, Georgekutty fail to notice Rajan's hardship, turning Rajan against him.
Georgekutty is deeply shaken by this episode when Rani mentions noticing someone at their plantation. Georgekutty inspects the area and discovers that a pit was dug and then filled. He digs the same spot but finds nothing all the while being secretly watched by Sahadevan. 
After being suspended, Sahadevan became a drunkard who will do anything for money. He retraces Georgekutty's steps in an attempt to find Varun's skeleton and get Georgekutty arrested, thereby getting back at him. Sahadevan approaches Dr. Ranjini, the psychiatrist for Anju, and demands Anju's file be handed over. Ranjini refuses but Sahadevan turns violent and gets the file and asks for some changes to be made. He threatens Pathrose as well and gets the identity of the skeleton he retrieved and gave to Georgekutty to replace Varun's with. The activities of Sahadevan are told to IG Thomas Bastin by Sabu, who was approached by Sahadevan to get details on Varun's case. As a result, Sahadevan is questioned by Bastin but he refuses to cooperate, prompting Bastin to order his arrest. At this juncture, Bastin is approached by Prabhakar, who acquaints him with the condition of Geetha, who had fallen into depression.
After completing Varun's last rites, Geetha was consumed by regret for being unable to bring justice to her son and the feeling that even her own husband did not side with her. Her condition gradually worsened, with her not talking to anyone or taking food properly. The fear of losing Geetha as well convinces Prabhakar to turn against Georgekutty and family, who, he feels, did not face any consequences and are leading a happy life. He hires Sahadevan to find evidences against Georgekutty. It was as part of this plan, Sahadevan and his daughter Yami started collecting details on Georgekutty. Prabhakar convinces Bastin to join them, promising to give him the glory if their operation turns out to be a success. 
Meanwhile, Anju's marriage is fixed with Avira, an NRI. Georgekutty purposefully looked for such a groom so that Anju can be send abroad, ensuring her safety. Prabhakar and Bastin tries their best to get Georgekutty and family arrested and get the wedding cancelled, but Georgekutty, through Adv. Renuka, thwarts their attemps and the wedding takes place. However, Prabhakar dismisses Georgekutty's escape till now as just luck and concludes he would not have been able to do anything if not for the adequate time he received. To prove his point, he narrates to Bastin how he, in a similar fashion, had anticipated all this. He reveals that he made Avira his man by saving him from debt. He gave the brokers money and sent them to Georgekutty and made him accept the proposal with Avira. 
Prabhakar reveals his plan. Avira will take Anju to a resort, where she will be be drugged. Sahadevan will then enter and together they will create a scene to make it appear as if Anju attacked Avira. Sahadevan will then call Georgekutty and after administering the drugs to awaken Anju, they will flee the scene. When Georgekutty reaches the location, police officers will arrive and he and Anju will be taken to custody. With the use of Anju's pyschiatry files, which have been tampered to establish that she has Sexual Aversion Syndrome and that she will get violent if somone makes advances, it will be made so that the condition was triggered when Avira approached her and as a result he was killed and the body was desposed by Georgekutty and Anju. It will then be argued that the same happened with Varun, thereby leading to their arrests while Avira escapes to Tunisia. 
However, the day after, Bastin informs Prabhakar that Sahadevan had other plans. It appears that when Sahadevan and Avira was about to create the scene, Anju was accidently awakened. The duo escapes but not before Sahadevan seriously injures Anju. Georgekutty and police officers arrive to find an unconscious Anju. Bastin suspects that Sahadevan might have actually murdered Avira to make the case stronger. As Prabhakar and Bastin searches for Sahadevan, they are confronted by Georgekutty. He reveals that whilst he was travelling to Anju after receiving the call from Sahadevan, it dawned on him that the pit on his plantation was made by Sahadevan and that he is planning to bury Avira there. Through his brother-in-law Rajesh, Georgekutty makes people gather at the plantation, thereby thwarting the plan of Sahadevan and through Jose, he finds where Sahadevan is hiding. He arrives to Sahadevan, severely beats him up and takes a confession video from him, making Prabhakar and Bastin's roles evident.
Georgekutty decides to surrender to the police and approaches Bastin along with Renuka to present his conditions. Prabhakar arrives to the scene, where he tells Georgekutty that he is least bothered by the confession videa and that he intends to continue hunting his daughter. In response, Georgekutty tells that wants to end the war here and that he will kill him if anything happens to his daughter. As they leave, Renuka asks Georgekutty to reconsider his decision, where he reveals he is not afriad of others but himself at this point. It was he who attacked his daughter and put the blame on Sahadevan to save her. Prabhakar and Bastin realises the same through Avira. Bastin tells Prabhakar how it was not luck that brought him till here.
As Georgekutty's surrender is being telecasted, Prabhakar asks Geetha whether she is happy now. She replies in the negative, wanting to take Anju as well.

== Production ==


=== Development ===
Jeethu Joseph stated that the idea for a third part came to him after completing Drishyam 2, beginning with the climax that first took shape in his mind. During the film's release, when Mohanlal asked whether there was a possibility of another sequel, he shared with him the envisioned climax of the potential continuation, which Mohanlal liked and encouraged Jeethu to develop further. In February 2021, shortly after the release of Drishyam 2, producer Antony Perumbavoor told Manorama News that Jeethu was contemplating about Drishyam 3 and had discussed it with Mohanlal, expressing hope that it would happen. A few days later, Jeethu told The Quint, "if I get a thread good enough to explore, I will start developing it. It will be a crime if I don't". At the Mazhavil Entertainment Awards in August 2022, Antony confirmed that Drishyam 3 would "definitely happen" and they were working on ideas. In November that year, Jeethu stated that he had "something in mind. However, I'm still looking for the spark to move it forward".
In a March 2024 interview with Deccan Chronicle, Jeethu said he was exploring the possibility of a sequel and would proceed only if he found a good idea, stressing that he would never make a sequel purely for financial gain. In August 2024, during an interview with Red FM, he revealed that he was stuck on a particular area of the story, though he already knew how the film would end. On 20 February 2025, Drishyam 3 was officially announced by Mohanlal through his social media handles.
Jeethu also revealed that Drishyam 3 would not serve as a conclusion to Georgekutty's story. However, in February 2026, he stated that he is trying to end the series. In April 2026, Pen Studios published a statement announcing that they have invested ₹100 crore in Aashirvad Cinemas through Panorama Studios for Drishyam 3, without revealing further. Pen and Panorama received "presented by" credits in the film.


=== Filming ===
Principal photography began on 22 September 2025 at Sree Narayana Law College, Poothotta, Ernakulam, with a traditional pooja ceremony. Filming took place across Thodupuzha, Vagamon, Ernakulam and Thiruvalla. Filming was completed on 2 December 2025.


== Release ==


=== Theatrical ===
Drishyam 3 was released worldwide on 21 May 2026, coinciding with Mohanlal's birthday. Earlier, the film was planned for release on 2 April 2026, but was postponed due to the 2026 Iran war.


=== Distribution ===
Panorama Studios and Pen Studios jointly bought the theatrical distribution rights of the film. Phars Film made a distribution partnership with Panorama Studios.


=== Home Media ===
In April 2026, Amazon Prime Video claimed to be the sole holder of the film's post-theatrical streaming rights, stating that the rights had been acquired through a valid agreement with the makers. The statement followed reports that the makers were negotiating streaming deals with other third parties. Subsequently, Amazon Prime Video filed a petition at the Delhi High Court, which granted interim relief restraining the makers from entering into agreements with third parties for the creation or exploitation of streaming rights related to the Drishyam film series.


== Reception ==
Rating 4 out of 5 stars, Suchin Mehrotra of The Quint described it as "a solid third chapter" and "a worthy sequel and continuation of Georgekutty’s journey, and I can’t think of a greater compliment". Sia Viju of Mathrubhumi, who also gave 4 out of 5 stars, noted that "Jeethu Joseph adopts a slightly different narrative rhythm here, choosing to explore emotional repercussions rather than purely relying on twists or procedural drama". Vishal Menon of The Hollywood Reporter India called the film a "mostly satisfying conclusion", noting that "the storytelling elevates Drishyam 3 beyond thriller territory into a complex morality tale".
Calling it "a hattrick", Athulya Nambiar of Mid-Day remarked that "it may seem that the twists and turns in the third film are not as elaborate or impactful as those in the earlier instalments. But that is precisely the point. Unlike the previous films, Georgekutty is never entirely in control of the narrative in Drishyam 3. He is constantly reacting to situations rather than orchestrating them". Bhawana Tanmayi of Moneycontrol rated 3.5 out of 5 stars and wrote that "the film has enough suspense and drama to satisfy fans of the franchise thanks to Mohanlal’s commanding performance and Jeethu Joseph’s storytelling".
Vignesh Madhu of The New Indian Express gave 3 out of 5 stars, describing it as "short on thrills, not soul". The film has "Mohanlal shining the best as the vulnerable Georgekutty, while Jeethu Joseph’s writing takes a hit despite some brave calls". Divya P. of Deccan Herald rated 3 out of 5 stars, stating that the "credit goes to Jeethu for creating Georgekutty, for whom the audience still roots for. Mohanlal ably anchors the film with his compelling performance", although she felt "Drishyam 3 does not quite meet the standards set by its predecessors". Anjana George of The Times of India awarded 3 out of 5 stars and wrote: "Jeethu Joseph attempts to explore guilt, paranoia, and psychological exhaustion more than suspense this time. The focus is not entirely on investigation or survival, but on how violence silently continues to exist inside relationships, memory, and everyday life".
Latha Srinivasan of NDTV gave 2.5 out of 5 stars, writing that "Drishyam 3 is not as thrilling and grilling as the previous two films but it is a must watch for Mohanlal and what Georgekutty represents". Janani K. of India Today gave 2.5 out of 5 stars, describing it as "a sequel that prioritises drama over intelligence. While the film has some meaty ideas that could make for a brilliant thriller, it settles for less because of its writing", but praised the actors' performances.
Gayathri Kallukaran of the Eastern Eye wrote that "Drishyam 3 may not deliver the Georgekutty many expected, but it offers a version of the character shaped by years of fear, guilt and survival. That change is likely to define much of the conversation around the film". Anandu Suresh of The Indian Express gave 2.5 out of 5 stars, writing that the "script feels all over the place, evidently torn between the writer-director's attempts to make it both an emotional drama and a crime thriller". However, "only Mohanlal delivers a performance worth mentioning. He highlights the many layers of Georgekutty's psyche with finesse, especially in the climax".
S. R. Praveen of The Hindu wrote that "Mohanlal’s interpretation of Georgekutty’s character saves the film from getting completely bogged down by director Jeethu Joseph’s outdated visual style and heavy-handed drama". Neeshita Nyayapati of the Hindustan Times gave 2 out of 5 stars, calling it "the weakest in Jeethu Joseph's hit franchise. It struggles even with its much-hyped twists".


== Remake ==
The Hindi remake of the same name, directed by Abhishek Pathak, is scheduled to be released on 2 October 2026.
